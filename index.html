About

Barangay Bigte is a rural community located in the municipality of Norzagaray, in the province of Bulacan, Philippines. It is situated on the eastern side of Norzagaray, bordering the province of Rizal. The barangay is named after the Tagalog word "bigte" which means "to interrupt" or "to intersect," as it is located at the intersection of several major roads.

Barangay Bigte is a relatively small barangay with a population of around 5,000 people. Most of the residents are engaged in agriculture, with rice being the primary crop. There are also some small-scale businesses and services in the area, such as sari-sari stores, carinderias, and repair shops.

The barangay is served by a number of public facilities, including an elementary school, a barangay health center, and a covered court. The local government also provides various programs and services to the community, such as feeding programs for children, health services, and skills training.

Despite being a rural area, Barangay Bigte is accessible by public transportation. Buses and jeepneys regularly pass through the barangay, providing easy access to nearby towns and cities. Overall, Barangay Bigte is a peaceful and friendly community with a strong sense of unity among its residents.
